What is an Age in Place Curbless Shower?

A curbless shower is an essential part of any open concept design. They are easy to clean, easily accessible, and give your bathroom a more spacious and modern feel. There are a few drawbacks to getting a curbless shower, but, for most people, the pros outweigh the cons by a long shot.

What is an age in place curbless shower exactly? Just what the name suggests: a shower without a threshold. It exists seamlessly with the bathroom floor. This means that it is easy for people of all ages to access the shower, making it an excellent long term option for your bathroom.

Considerations and Construction

The primary factor to consider in a curbless shower’s construction is drainage. A poorly planned curbless shower can result in a watery mess for your bathroom. At Dream Build, we use Schluter’s KERDI offering of products to ensure your bathroom is properly waterproofed and drained, so this won’t be an issue. We also use DITRA and DITRA-HEAT for our flooring, which prevents the main cause of cracked tile and grout and can add comfortable electric floor warming.

Generally, there are three ways to construct your curbless shower. One method is to use a recessed floor (see the first video below). If a recessed floor is not possible for your shower, you can also use a prefabricated shower tray and ramp. Lastly, if neither of those options are possible, you can choose to raise the bathroom floor. Most bathrooms will be the able to accommodate at least one of these methods of installation.
